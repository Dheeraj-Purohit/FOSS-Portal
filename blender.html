Blender

Introduction

Blender is a free and open-source 3D creation suite used for modeling, animation, rendering, simulation, video editing, and game development. It is one of the most advanced FOSS projects, competing directly with expensive commercial software like Maya and 3ds Max. Blender empowers artists, developers, and studios worldwide.

History

Blender was originally developed in 1994 by Ton Roosendaal as an in-house tool for a Dutch animation studio. It became open-source in 2002 after a crowdfunding campaign called "Free Blender." Since then, it has grown exponentially, with frequent updates and contributions from both developers and studios.

Features

  • Comprehensive 3D pipeline: modeling, sculpting, animation
  • Cycles and Eevee rendering engines
  • Video editing and compositing tools
  • Game engine and physics simulations
  • Large community and thousands of tutorials

How to Download

Blender is available for Windows, macOS, and Linux. It can be downloaded freely from its official website. No subscription or license fee is required, making it accessible to hobbyists and professionals alike.
