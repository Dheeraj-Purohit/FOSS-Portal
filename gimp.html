GIMP

Introduction

GIMP (GNU Image Manipulation Program) is a free and open-source raster graphics editor. It is widely used for tasks such as photo retouching, image composition, and graphic design. Often considered the free alternative to Adobe Photoshop, GIMP is highly customizable with plugins and scripts.

History

GIMP was first released in 1996 by Spencer Kimball and Peter Mattis as a university project. Over time, it became one of the most respected tools in the FOSS world. Its longevity and continuous updates have made it popular among artists, photographers, and web designers.

Features

  • Advanced image editing and retouching tools
  • Supports layers, masks, and filters
  • Customizable interface
  • Cross-platform availability
  • Large plugin ecosystem

How to Download

GIMP can be downloaded free of cost from its official website. It is available for Windows, Linux, and macOS. Many Linux distributions also include GIMP in their package repositories.
