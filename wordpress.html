WordPress

Introduction

WordPress is the world’s most popular free and open-source content management system (CMS). It powers more than 40% of all websites on the internet, from personal blogs to major news outlets. WordPress allows users to build websites without needing deep coding knowledge.

History

WordPress was launched in 2003 by Matt Mullenweg and Mike Little as a fork of an older blogging software called b2/cafelog. Over the years, it has grown into a full-featured CMS with thousands of themes and plugins. Today, WordPress has one of the largest developer and user communities in the open-source world.

Features

  • User-friendly and beginner-friendly
  • Thousands of free and premium themes
  • Extensive plugin ecosystem
  • SEO-friendly and mobile-ready
  • Supports blogs, business sites, and e-commerce

How to Download

WordPress can be downloaded from its official website or installed via one-click installers on most hosting providers. It requires PHP and MySQL/MariaDB to run and can be easily deployed on Linux servers.
