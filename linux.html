Linux

Introduction

Linux is one of the most influential FOSS projects in history. It is an open-source operating system kernel created by Linus Torvalds in 1991. Unlike traditional commercial operating systems, Linux is developed and maintained collaboratively by programmers around the world. Today, Linux is the backbone of servers, smartphones, embedded devices, cloud platforms, and even supercomputers. Its flexibility allows it to be adapted for multiple purposes — from personal computing to enterprise-level solutions.

History

The Linux project started as a hobby by Linus Torvalds while studying at the University of Helsinki. Initially released as a free alternative to MINIX, it quickly attracted developers globally who began improving and expanding it. Over the years, Linux distributions (distros) such as Ubuntu, Fedora, Debian, and Arch Linux emerged, making it accessible to users of all skill levels.

Features

  • Completely free and open-source
  • Highly secure and stable
  • Customizable for any need
  • Supports a wide range of hardware
  • Massive community support

How to Download

You can download different Linux distributions depending on your needs. Ubuntu is beginner-friendly, Fedora is developer-focused, while Debian and Arch cater to advanced users. Each distribution provides installation images and detailed guides.
