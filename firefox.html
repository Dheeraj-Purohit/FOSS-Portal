Mozilla Firefox

Introduction

Mozilla Firefox is a free and open-source web browser developed by the Mozilla Foundation. Known for its speed, security, and flexibility, Firefox has been a major competitor to proprietary browsers for decades. It provides users with strong privacy features, open standards support, and a highly customizable interface.

History

Firefox was first released in 2002 under the name "Phoenix." Its goal was to provide a lightweight, open alternative to bloated web browsers like Internet Explorer. By 2004, Firefox had already gained millions of users, becoming a symbol of the open-source movement. Today, it continues to evolve with frequent updates and strong community support.

Features

  • Fast and lightweight
  • Strong privacy protection (Enhanced Tracking Protection)
  • Customizable with themes and extensions
  • Cross-platform support
  • Open standards and developer-friendly tools

How to Download

Firefox is available for Windows, macOS, Linux, and Android. It can be downloaded directly from the Mozilla website and comes with easy installation instructions. Users can also install it from package managers in Linux distributions.
